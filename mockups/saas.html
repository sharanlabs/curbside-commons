The problem

The merchants you signed up but never got live are lost revenue.

A marketplace's pre-live merchants represent real, recoverable GMV — but the long tail is far too large to work by hand, and merchants stall at different points for different reasons. Reactivation outreach fails for one reason above all: it's generic instead of matched to the actual blocker.

Real lost GMV, hiding in the long tail

Signed-up-but-never-live merchants are recoverable revenue — but there are too many to reach manually, one at a time.

They stall at different steps

Verify business → menu → photos → hours → banking → final check. Every stall point needs a different next move.

Generic outreach is the #1 failure

A message that ignores the real blocker doesn't convert — and the AI-outreach wave makes it worse by fabricating claims at scale.

~74% stall

The onboarding funnel leaks at every step

Each stage loses merchants. What survives is a long, thin tail of stalls — each one fixable, but only with the right next message.

The pipeline

Six steps. The risky calls are code; the AI only drafts.

The spine of the product — deterministic where it must be auditable, bounded where the AI helps, and human-owned where it's irreversible.

1

Triage

Find who's stuck, and how badly.

Deterministic risk score + blocker. Auditable.

2

Diagnose

Pin the exact blocker and the play.

Engagement state + root cause; play varies by why.

3

Draft

Write the right next message.

Bounded, schema-constrained LLM. Single-shot.

4

Gate

Check each declared claim vs. the data.

Claims-gatekeeper + forbidden-claim guardrails.

5

Score

Measure draft quality.

Deterministic graders: structure · state · policy.

6

Approve

A human decides: hold / reject / send.

Human-in-the-loop · audit trail · $5 cost ledger.

Trust & safety

A direct antidote to the AI-outreach failure frontier.

False claims, churn, and regulatory liability are exactly what the AI-outreach wave is hitting. The architecture is built against those failures — not around them.

Deterministic makes the risky call

Risk, blocker, eligibility, and send/hold are pure code — reproducible and replayable. The AI never decides who to contact or whether to send.

replayableregression-tested

The AI is bounded, never autonomous

Schema-constrained, single-shot drafting behind guardrails — then evaluated. No agentic loop, no tool autonomy, no unbounded spend.

schema-constrainedevaluated

Every declared claim traces to data

The claims-gatekeeper ties each declared claim to the merchant's own structured data, with forbidden-claim guardrails — before any person sees the draft.

claim → dataforbidden-claim guard

A human owns every irreversible send

High-risk drafts are held for a person; only clean, low-risk ones simulate-send. A human gate is the practitioner-validated control.

hold / reject / sendaudit trail

Spend hard-capped at $5, fail-closed

A per-call and cumulative budget guard blocks anything that would breach the cap. An unknown model fails loud — never silently bills $0.

≤ $5.00cumulative ledger

The public demo is REPLAY-only

No live calls on the public surface — it renders a recorded run. Zero spend, reproducible, and safe to deploy or share.

no live callsrecorded fixture
