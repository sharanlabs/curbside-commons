01The problemLost activation

A marketplace's biggest leak isn't churn.
It's the merchants who never go live.

Every signed-up merchant who stalls mid-onboarding is booked revenue that never arrives. The list is enormous, the work is manual, and the outreach that does go out mostly misses — because it's generic.

a.

Signed up, never live

Pre-live merchants are real lost GMV — committed supply that the marketplace already paid to acquire, sitting idle.

b.

A long tail too big to work by hand

Thousands of small accounts. No team can read every one, find the blocker, and write a fitting message at that scale.

c.

They stall mid-flow

Verify business → menu → photos → hours → banking → final check. Most drop between two specific steps and stay there.

d.

Generic outreach fails

The #1 reason reactivation misses: the message isn't matched to the actual blocker. A nudge about photos can't move a banking stall.

So the real task isn't "send more."
It's match the message to the blocker — at scale, without lying.

02The pipelineSix steps, one spine

One disciplined path from a stalled
account to a send a human approved.

Deterministic code makes the risky call first. The model only writes inside a box. A gatekeeper checks the claims, an eval scores the draft, and a person decides. Read it left to right.

01

Triage

Find who's stuck, and how badly.

Deterministic risk score + blocker level from an auditable formula. The risky ranking call is made by code you can replay, not by a model.

02

Diagnose

Pin the exact blocker, and the right play.

Engagement state + root cause → a reactivation play that varies by why they're stuck, not just which step they're on.

03

Draft

Write the right next message.

A bounded, schema-constrained LLM writes inside a fixed structure. The untrusted merchant name never reaches the model. (REPLAY here; a real Gemini run is recorded.)

04

Gate

Check each declared claim against the data.

A claims-gatekeeper ties every declared claim to the merchant's own data, plus forbidden-claim guardrails. (Undeclared prose isn't fully semantically verified — a documented boundary.)

05

Score

Measure the draft's quality.

Deterministic eval graders over structure · state-consistency · policy — so quality is a number, not a vibe.

06

Approve

A human decides: hold / reject / send.

A human-in-the-loop gate on every irreversible send, a full audit trail, and a $5 fail-closed cost ledger behind it all.

What the instrument did — and where it stopped.

  1. 01Triaged High. The deterministic score ranked this account high-risk from its stalled, pre-live state — an auditable, replayable call.
  2. 02Diagnosed the blocker. Root cause = business verification. The play is matched to that exact blocker, not a generic "come back" nudge.
  3. 03Drafted in a box. The bounded model wrote a "verify your business" message inside a fixed schema — with the merchant name kept out of the prompt.
  4. 04Gated the claims. Each declared claim was traced back to the merchant's own data; forbidden promises were screened. All clean.
  5. 05Stopped at the human. Even with a clean draft, nothing sends itself. It's held for review — a person decides hold, reject, or send.

Fog City Tacos is a fictional merchant and the activation state is synthetic — shown to demonstrate the flow, not a real account.

04Why it's safe to adoptThe trust model

A direct antidote to the AI-outreach
failure frontier.

The wave of autonomous AI outreach is hitting false claims, churn, and regulatory liability. This is built the opposite way: the model never makes the risky call, and never sends.

Deterministic makes the risky call

The triage and ranking are plain code — same inputs, same output, every time. You can replay any decision.

The AI is bounded and evaluated

The model writes inside a schema and every draft is scored by graders. It's never autonomous.

Every declared claim traces to data

Each declared claim in a message is tied back to the merchant's own record, with forbidden-claim guardrails on top.

A human owns every send

Every irreversible send waits on a person — hold, reject, or send — with a full audit trail behind the decision.

Spend is hard-capped at $5

A fail-closed cost ledger stops the run before it can breach the cap. Cost is a guardrail, not a surprise.

The public demo is REPLAY-only

No live calls in the demo — it replays a recorded run. Spend stays $0 while you read it.

05Why it generalizesThe transferable spine

Merchant activation is one instance.
The spine is the product.

Strip out "merchant" and the same disciplined path remains. It transfers to any "use AI to act on people or accounts at scale, safely" problem — wherever a wrong, autonomous message would cost real money or trust.

deterministic triage→ bounded LLM→ claims-gate→ eval→ human approval→ audit
  • FintechDunning & collections — act on at-risk accounts without overstating balances or terms.
  • SaaSTrial & expansion nudges — message on real usage signals, never invented ones.
  • HealthCare-gap outreach — claims traced to the record; a clinician owns the send.
  • SupportProactive resolution — drafts checked against ticket data before a human approves.
