01 / THE READING

A mismatch you can see before you read a word.

A claim arrives looking perfectly well-formed. The gauge reads it against the merchant’s own record, and where the two disagree, the light itself breaks pattern — the difference is visible before any receipt is opened. When the reading settles, the result stays on the bench, complete.

02 / THE METHOD

Four parts, kept attached.

THE CLAIM

A listing is a set of statements — a price, an availability, a name. The instrument treats each one as a claim to be read, not a fact to be believed.

03 / THE BOUNDARY

An instrument that knows its edges.

It checks what it can see.

The gauge reads only the inputs it is given. It does not guess at missing facts, and it says so when a question is outside the record in front of it.

Unresolved stays visible.

When the evidence cannot settle a question, the reading is held open — plainly marked — rather than rounded off to a pass or a fail.

Structure is not truth.

A perfectly valid document can still misstate the world. Format checks and truth checks are kept apart, and named apart.

Every call shows its work.

Any conclusion the instrument draws can be traced back through rule, record, and claim — and re-drawn by the reader.
