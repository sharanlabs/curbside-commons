01 / the problem

A marketplace's signed-up-but-never-live merchants are real lost revenue.

Every merchant who completes sign-up but never goes live is GMV that was promised and never collected. The long tail is too big to work by hand — and merchants don't stall in one place. They drop off mid-onboarding, anywhere along the path.

root cause of failed outreach

The #1 reason reactivation outreach fails isn't effort. It's being generic instead of matched to the merchant's actual blocker.

A "finish setting up your store" blast to a merchant stuck on bank verification is noise. The fix isn't more outreach — it's outreach that knows why.

02 / the pipeline

One spine, six steps.
The risky call is deterministic. The writing is bounded.

Watch it draw itself. Each node hands a typed payload to the next — deterministic code does the auditable work, the model writes inside a schema, and a human owns the send.

01 — Triage
deterministic

A score you can replay.

A deterministic risk score and a single named blocker. No model in the loop — the same merchant always grades the same way, every run.

02 — Diagnose
deterministic

Why they're stuck.

Engagement state plus root cause, mapped to a reactivation play that varies by why — not just where in the funnel they fell off.

03 — Draft
bounded llm

The model writes — inside a box.

A schema-constrained LLM produces the message. It can only fill declared fields against a fixed structure. It cannot invent the call, the score, or the send.

04 — Gate
deterministic

Every claim, checked.

A claims-gatekeeper checks each declared claim against the merchant's own data, plus forbidden-claim guardrails. Unsupported claim → blocked, not sent.

05 — Score
deterministic

Graded before anyone sees it.

Deterministic eval graders check structure, state-consistency, and policy. Quality is measured — not assumed — on every draft.

06 — Approve
human

A person owns the send.

A human decides hold, reject, or send. Every step is captured in a full audit trail, with a $5 fail-closed cost ledger behind it.

04 / why you can trust it

The risky decision is code. The model is on a leash.

Every property below exists so that "use AI to reach people at scale" doesn't become "let a model say untrue things to your merchants."

Deterministic makes the call

The risk score and blocker come from code, not a model. Replayable, inspectable, identical run over run.

replayable · no model in triage

The AI is bounded

Schema-constrained and eval-graded, never autonomous. The model fills fields; it doesn't make decisions.

bounded · evaluated · not autonomous

Every claim traces to data

If a sentence makes a claim, that claim maps to a field the merchant actually has — or it's blocked.

claim → field, or no send

A human owns the send

Every irreversible action waits for a person. The system holds; it never sends on its own.

hold · reject · send — by a human

Spend is hard-capped

A $5 fail-closed cost ledger sits under the run. If accounting breaks, it stops — it does not overspend.

$5 cap · fail-closed

The demo is replay-only

The public walkthrough makes no live calls — it replays a recorded run, so what you see is exactly reproducible.

replay-only · no live calls
