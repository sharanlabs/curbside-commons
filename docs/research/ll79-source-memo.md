## 1 · Answer-first summary (Pyramid)

- **Operative section:** NYC Administrative Code **§ 20-563.3 "Fee caps"**, as amended by **Local Law 79 of 2025** (Council **Int. No. 762-B of 2024**). LL79 also amends **§ 20-563** (definitions).
- **Effective date — CONFLICT RESOLVED: the amendments took effect 2025-06-30.** The "2025-05-31" that secondary sources report is the **enactment date** (the day the law *became* law), **not** the effective date. LL79 § 4 sets effect at **"30 days after it becomes law"**; 2025-05-31 + 30 days = **2025-06-30**. See §3.
- **Rule shape:** a four-category fee scheme, each with a percentage cap on the **purchase price of each online order**, with a calendar-month averaging alternative on three of the four:
  - delivery fee ≤ **15%** (§20-563.3(a))
  - basic service fee ≤ **5%** (§20-563.3(b))
  - transaction fee ≤ **3%**, with a documented pass-through exception (§20-563.3(c))
  - enhanced service fee ≤ **20%**, chargeable **only if** a basic service fee is also offered (§20-563.3(d))
  - any fee outside these four categories is **unlawful** (§20-563.3(d) opening clause).
- **Audit-relevant machinery:** a monthly over-cap **refund safe harbor** (subd. e — covers a, b, d only, **not** c), mandatory **monthly itemized transaction statements** (subd. h), and **fee-disclosure / change-notification** duties (subd. g).
- **Penalty/restitution amounts are NOT in §20-563.3** — they live elsewhere in Subchapter 36 (not extracted this pass). Marked UNVERIFIED (§5).

## 2 · Exact rule-text references (primary, verbatim)

Primary text below is quoted from the **enacted local law PDF** (City Clerk certified copy), retrieved as a PDF and extracted with `pdftotext` (poppler 26.06.0). Source S1.

**LL79 § 3 amends § 20-563.3. Verbatim clauses (load-bearing):**

- **(a) delivery fee — 15%:** *"It shall be unlawful for a third-party food delivery service to charge a food service establishment a delivery fee that totals more than 15% of the purchase price of each online order unless the average of the sum of all delivery fees … in a calendar month totals no more than 15% of the sum of the purchase price of all such orders in such month."* Plus a delivery obligation: *"shall provide delivery to any customer located within at least 1 mile … unless exigent circumstances prevent such delivery."*
- **(b) basic service fee — 5%:** *"a basic service fee that totals more than 5% of the purchase price of each online order unless the average … in a calendar month totals no more than 5% …"* (The bracketed old text — *"any fee or fees, other than a delivery fee and a transaction fee, for the use of their service"* — is shown struck, i.e., **deleted** by LL79.)
- **(c) transaction fee — 3% + pass-through exception:** *"a transaction fee that totals more than 3% of the purchase price of each online order, provided however that a third-party food delivery service may charge … more than 3% … if: (i) such transaction fee is charged … in the same amount as the charge imposed upon the third-party food delivery service for such online order, and (ii) such third-party food delivery service can provide proof of such charge … to both the department and the relevant food service establishment upon request."*
- **(d) category lock + enhanced service fee — 20%:** *"It shall be unlawful … to charge … any fee other than a basic service fee, delivery fee or transaction fee, except that a third-party food delivery service that offers a service for which such … service charges a basic service fee pursuant to this section may charge an enhanced service fee. Such an enhanced service fee may total no more than 20% of the purchase price of each online order, unless the average … in a calendar month totals no more than 20% …"*
- **(e) over-cap refund safe harbor:** *"it shall not be a violation [of] this section where a third-party food delivery service refunds … the portion of any fee … that exceeds any fee cap imposed pursuant to subdivisions a, b or d of this section within 30 calendar days of the final day of the month in which such charge occurs."* (Note: **subdivision c is not listed** — the transaction-fee cap has no refund safe harbor.)
- **(f) search/discoverability for basic-service payers:** *"such third-party food delivery service shall enable a person to search for such food service establishment on all websites and mobile and online platforms … and shall not omit such food service establishment from relevant search results …"*
- **(g) notification + disclosure:** (1) within 30 days of the effective date, notify existing establishments of (i) services offered, (ii) **applicable caps under subdivisions a, b, and d**, (iii) services under the existing agreement, (iv) any fee change (effective **no earlier than 30 days** after notice); (2) establishment may modify services after a fee-change notice or an enhanced-service offer; (3) *"shall disclose, in a clear and conspicuous manner, any fee, commission, or other charge imposed …"*
- **(h) monthly itemized statement:** *"Each month, a third-party food delivery service shall provide to a food service establishment … an itemized list of each transaction … including any fees such … service charged … in the preceding month."*
- **(i)** keyword-advertising consent restriction; **(j)** must allow menus/coupons/marketing inserts with orders; **(k)** anti-price-parity (no forced menu-price matching as a condition of service); **(l)** commissioner fee-cap report due **no later than Sept 30, 2026** (enumerated factors 1–8, incl. violations by section, penalties imposed, restitution recovered).

**LL79 §§ 1–2 amend § 20-563 (definitions):**
- **delivery fee** (amended): *"a fee charged … for providing a food service establishment with a service that delivers food from such establishment to customers located within at least 1 mile …"* (the prior carve-out sentence excluding listing/advertising/processing fees is **deleted**).
- **basic service fee** (new): a service that (i) *"allows such food service establishment to receive orders for delivery and pick-up on all websites and mobile and online platforms …"* and (ii) *"enables a person to search for such food service establishment … in a manner that prominently displays such food service establishment by name and includes [it] in results for a search based on customer criteria, such as cuisine type, location, or delivery radius …"*
- **enhanced service fee** (new): *"a fee … for providing a food service establishment with a service other than the service such … service provides … in exchange for paying a basic service fee, a delivery fee, or a transaction fee."*
- **exigent circumstances** (new): *"circumstances outside the control of a third-party food delivery service, such as severe weather conditions or natural disasters."*

## 3 · Effective-date conflict — RESOLVED

**The conflict (from plan §9 O4 and pivot-research §D):** secondary sources split between **2025-05-31** and **2025-06-30** as the "effective date" of LL79.

**Resolution: the amendments are EFFECTIVE 2025-06-30. "2025-05-31" is the *enactment* (became-law) date, not the effective date.** The two are exactly 30 days apart by the law's own terms.

**Evidence chain (primary → confirming):**
1. **Primary — LL79 § 4 (verbatim, S1):** *"§ 4. This local law takes effect 30 days after it becomes law."* So the effective date = (became-law date) + 30 days.
2. **Primary — City Clerk certification on the enacted LL79 (S1):** *"passed by the Council on May 1, 2025 and returned unsigned by the Mayor on June 2, 2025."* Under the NYC Charter, a local law neither signed nor vetoed within 30 days of presentation **becomes law by operation of the Charter**; the Council's own record labels this the **"City Charter Rule Adopted"** event.
3. **Legislative record — Legistar, Int 0762-2024 action history (S3):** **Enactment date = 5/31/2025**; **Law number = 2025/079**; **"City Charter Rule Adopted" = 5/31/2025**; **"Returned Unsigned by Mayor" = 6/2/2025**. So the law **became law on 2025-05-31**; the "returned unsigned" clerical entry on the next business day (2025-06-02; 5/31 was a Saturday) is administrative, not the legal enactment date.
4. **Confirming secondary #1 — intro.nyc official local-law metadata (S1 landing / S2):** LL 2025/79 **effective 6/30/2025**.
5. **Confirming secondary #2 — amlegal codification note surfaced in search (S4):** *"Local Law 79 of 2025 was effective as of 6/30/2025."*

**Arithmetic check:** enactment 2025-05-31 + 30 days = **2025-06-30**. Two independent secondary sources report exactly this. **The "2025-05-31" figure in the earlier digest is the enactment date mislabeled "effective."** Confidence: **HIGH** (primary effective-clause + primary legislative record + two independent secondary confirmations, all mutually consistent).

▸ *Plain: the law "became law" on May 31, 2025 (the mayor let it pass without signing). The text says it kicks in 30 days later — June 30, 2025. Sources that said "effective May 31" mixed up the day it became law with the day it started applying.*

## 4 · Applicability / timing windows (for statement auditing)

- **Caps apply to charges on/after 2025-06-30.** Auditing a statement period straddling that date must apply the **pre-LL79** §20-563.3 (LL103/2021 baseline: 15% delivery + a single 5%-ish "other fees" cap, no formal enhanced tier) before 2025-06-30 and the **tiered LL79 scheme** on/after. (Pre-LL79 exact text NOT extracted this pass — UNVERIFIED, see §5.)
- **Monthly averaging window** (subd. a, b, d): a **calendar month** — the compliance denominator is *the sum of the purchase price of all such orders in such month*, not per-order, when the averaging alternative is used.
- **Refund safe harbor window** (subd. e): over-cap refunds must land **within 30 calendar days of the final day of the month in which the charge occurs** — so a month's over-cap charge is not a violation until that window closes. Audit logic must not flag an over-cap month as a violation before the safe-harbor window expires. **Applies to a, b, d only — never c.**
- **Fee-change notice** (subd. g(1)(iv)): a fee change takes effect **no earlier than 30 days** after the establishment is notified.
