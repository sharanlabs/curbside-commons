## 1. NARRATIVE SPINE

The story opens on an illustrative dinner order, then moves backward to the proof that must exist before an agent can place it: the menu makes a claim, the kitchen answers with its committed record, and the commons brings the difference into focus. The 100× price discrepancy establishes the stakes; the three-part method explains the comparison; the listings report shows its full consequence; and the fee-cap audit extends the same discipline to codified law. The limits remain visible—assumed fee basis, supplied-input boundary, and questions requiring external evidence—before the close returns proof to the agent without suggesting that Curbside Commons places the order itself.
